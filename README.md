# Thomas Cristancho

Researching why systems fail — and whether they were viable to begin with.

---

## Focus Areas

**Failure analysis in complex systems** — identifying the specific mechanism behind a failure, not just its symptom. Integration failures, silent execution errors, and structural ceilings each have a different shape.

**Feasibility boundaries** — determining what cannot work before investing in how to make it work. Some problems have mathematical constraints that no engineering solution can move.

**Execution realism** — the gap between theoretical models and live behavior. Cost assumptions, non-deterministic infrastructure, and environment mismatch each close that gap in different ways.

**System behavior under constraints** — how architectural decisions create residual failure patterns that cannot be resolved from inside the system that produced them.

## Philosophy

Discard fast. A direction that cannot work in principle should be identified through analysis, not through iteration. Feasibility testing should precede model development, not follow it. Constraints are primary signals: they define the boundaries of the tractable problem, and working inside those boundaries is more productive than working against them.

The most useful research finding is sometimes a definite no.
